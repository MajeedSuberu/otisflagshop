# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Swedish diaspora family members — grandparents, aunts, uncles, parents of Otis's extended network. They are not particularly tech-savvy. They may be older, reading on phones or laptops, often motivated by love rather than convenience. They are buying a keepsake, not a commodity. A Swedish farmor ordering from Addis Ababa is the defining user.

## Product Purpose

Otis is 6.5 years old and lives in Addis Ababa. He draws national flags by hand, and he calls every customer personally to confirm the order. He writes the delivery address himself on each package.

This page exists so that people who love Otis (or who hear about him) can order one of his flags. It is a shop, but it functions more like a bridge — between a child's creativity and pride, and the people far away who will receive something made by his hands.

Success is an order placed and a phone call anticipated with warmth.

## Brand Personality

Tender, sincere, handmade.

The voice is Otis's own — not cutesy, not performed. A 6.5-year-old who is genuinely proud of his craft. The page should feel like receiving a handwritten letter: personal, a little imperfect, completely real.

Emotional goal: make the buyer feel that something precious is coming to them — not a product, but a piece of a child's attention and care.

## Anti-references

- **Etsy / craft marketplace**: Generic handmade-goods aesthetic, beige rustic grids, marketplace distance between maker and buyer. This page is personal, not a platform.
- **Charity / NGO / humanitarian**: Polished, safe, cause-driven. This is not a fundraiser for a child. Otis is running a business he is proud of.
- **Kids' app / edtech**: Bright primary colors, bubbly fonts, talking-down-to-children tone. The page is about a child, not for children.
- **Generic e-commerce / Shopify**: Add-to-cart coldness, conversion optimization, product grids. The transaction here is almost ceremonial — it leads to a phone call.

## Design Principles

1. **Every detail signals a real person made this.** Not a brand, not a template. A child's shop. Imperfection is the feature — suppress the impulse to make things look finished.
2. **Warmth over polish.** Professionalism would be the wrong signal here. The page should feel like the package itself: hand-addressed, slightly tilted, full of care.
3. **The transaction is secondary to the relationship.** Otis calls you. The form is almost ceremonial. Design for that moment, not for conversion.
4. **Built for Swedish grandparents.** Legible at arm's length. Simple flow. No jargon, no assumptions about comfort with technology. If a farmor can't find the button, the design failed.
5. **Pride without performance.** Otis is serious about his art. The page celebrates craft without being precious or ironic about it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Larger base font sizes given the older audience. Good contrast throughout. Simple, linear flow — no multi-step wizards, no hidden states. Touch targets sized for people who don't use phones all day. Keyboard accessible for screen reader users.
